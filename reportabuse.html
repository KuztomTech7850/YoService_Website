Security & Abuse Reporting

If you’ve found a security issue or misuse of YoService systems, we want to hear from you. Responsible reports help us keep things safe for everyone.

  • How to report an issue

How to report a security issue

Please use one of the contact methods below to share as much detail as you reasonably can about the issue. Include what you observed, when it happened, and any steps to reproduce it.

  • Email: contact@yoservice.work (preferred)
  • Subject line suggestion: “Security report – YoService”
  • For urgent issues that could impact customer data or systems, you may also call us and mention that you’re reporting a security concern.

We currently do not run a formal bug bounty program, but we appreciate responsible disclosures and will treat all good‑faith reports with respect and confidentiality.

General security & abuse policy

YoService is a small, service‑focused business. We aim to keep our systems simple, secure, and aligned with how our customers actually use them.

We ask that anyone interacting with our website, forms, or any future YoService apps follows these basic principles:

  • Do not attempt to access data that does not belong to you or bypass authentication, rate limits, or other controls.
  • Do not use automated tools or bots to overload, scrape, or otherwise degrade our services.
  • Do not upload or transmit malicious content, including malware, phishing links, or spam.
  • If you discover a vulnerability, avoid sharing it publicly until we’ve had a reasonable chance to review and address it.

Abuse of our contact channels, forms, or any future platform features (including harassment, fraud, or misuse of automation) may result in blocks or other protective actions.

This page may evolve as YoService grows into a more app‑driven platform. When we introduce new features or APIs, we’ll update this policy so you always know how to report issues the right way.

Need to reach us directly?

If you’re unsure whether something counts as a security issue or abuse, reach out anyway. We’d rather review a cautious report than miss something important.
